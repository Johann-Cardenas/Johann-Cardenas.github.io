The engine

LEAPS solves the axisymmetric elasticity equations for an N-layer halfspace (Burmister's problem) with a Love stress function per layer in Hankel-transform space. Boundary conditions build a 4N−2 system per transform parameter, solved with scaled exponentials so no modulus ratio or layer thickness can overflow. Responses are recovered by adaptive panel quadrature between Bessel zeros with Wynn-ε acceleration; surface values use closed-form Weber–Schafheitlin tails, so deflection at z = 0 is exact to machine precision for a halfspace.

Validation

The engine ships with a regression suite of 69 checks: Boussinesq closed forms at all depths, multilayer reduction to the halfspace, spring-interface limits against fully bonded and frictionless solutions, vertical equilibrium, superposition symmetry, Burmister's two-layer deflection factor, and a 1000:1 modulus-contrast case with a 3 mm interlayer. A subset runs in your browser every time the app starts.

Conventions

Tension-positive stresses, z downward, deflection positive downward. Wheels are uniform circular pressures; contact radius follows from load and pressure. Bonded, frictionless, and shear-spring interfaces (k in MPa/mm) cover flexible, rigid, composite, and overlay systems. All quantities are shown in the unit system you pick — internally everything is mm · N · MPa.
